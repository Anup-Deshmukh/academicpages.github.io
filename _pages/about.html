Hello! I'm Anup, I currently reside in Bangalore, India where I'm majoring in computer science at IIIT-B (final year). Previously, I was part of FAST lab at CentraleSupelec, France in the summer of 2018. I am currently affiliated to the Multimodal Perception lab, led by Prof. Dinesh Babu.

I will be joining David R. Cheriton School of Computer Science at University of Waterloo from Fall 2019 where I will be working under the supervisoin of Prof. Ming Li as a graduate student.

I enjoy working on Deep learning methods that are especially applied to Recommender Systems and Computer Vision. You can find details of my Publications and Projects here.


My recent work "A Scalable Clustering Algorithm for Serendipity in Recommender Systems" is accepted in the Recommender Systmes track of ICDM 2018. My research focuses on building efficient and scalable Machine Learning algorithms for Recommender Systmes (RS) which go beyond accuracy. I'm currently working on my thesis where, broadly, my goal is to exploit Deep Learning methods to learn user-item interaction function in RS.

I am also interested in applications of Deep Learning to Computer Vision problems like crowd counting, image captioning and text-to-image synthesis. Building novel and practically useful Computer Vision algorithms has always excited me.


Apart from my work, I enjoy travelling and learning about new cultures. I love both online and offline reading. I am also an avid reader of world politics and get considerable enjoyment from sports.
